About Me

First Encounters with Technology

My journey with technology began when my dad introduced my siblings and I to our first family computer. I was immediately fascinated by how it worked and spent hours exploring its capabilities. This early exposure sparked a curiosity that would define my future path.

02

Childhood Inspirations

From late nights gaming to early mornings watching medical documentaries, I grew up equally inspired by the imaginative worlds of video games and the real-life heroes in white coats. Both sparked a fascination with systems—whether human or digital—and the desire to understand and improve them..

"

Stay curious—ask questions, follow wonder, and the answers will find you.

Early Creative Projects

By age 12 years old, I was already designing science fair displays that blended creativity with curiosity—constructing models of the human body, researching diseases, and presenting them with a passion that hinted at my future in health and science communication.

Education

Age 13 - Adulthood Primary School | Florida A&M University | Howard University
01

Formative School Years

During high school, I found myself captivated by the human body and the stories of people from around the world. My passions for medicine and cultural exploration grew side-by-side, shaping a worldview centered on empathy and understanding.

02

College Decisions

College was a journey of discovery—I changed majors three times, seeking clarity and purpose. Ultimately, I returned to my original path in pre-med, earning a Master of Public Health before culminating my academic journey with a Doctor of Pharmacy degree.

03

Key Academic Projects

Whether dissecting cadavers in gross anatomy, serving Native American communities on a reservation, or collaborating with the CDC as a pharmacy extern, I sought out experiences that pushed me beyond the classroom and into the heart of real-world public health.

Key Achievement

In 2020 despite a worldwide pandemic, I had the honor of presenting my own research at the American Pharmacists Association’s Midyear Conference, marking a major milestone in my growth as a clinical scholar and communicator.

Career Beginnings

First 5 Years Professional World
01

First Professional Role

My professional journey started in the ICU as a Cardiac Monitor Technician, where I first learned the value of vigilance, teamwork, and compassion. Every beep on the monitor told a story—and I learned to listen.

02

Major Projects & Learning

Working in patient care taught me that medicine is a team sport. Healing isn’t just clinical—it’s personal, educational, and deeply human. It was here I recognized that health literacy can be just as life-saving as any medication.

03

Growth & Development

Driven to make a broader impact, I transitioned from direct care to supporting communities affected by HIV, focusing on education, prevention, and access—recognizing that public health solutions must meet people where they are.

"

"In those early days, I learned that healing isn't just about medicine—it's about listening, teamwork, and meeting people where they are."

Pivotal Moments

Key Turning Points Career & Personal Life
01

Career Transformation

My path pivoted again as I returned to school to pursue a PharmD. Along the way, I uncovered a deep interest in health informatics—where data, technology, and clinical practice intersect to improve outcomes in innovative ways.

02

Taking Risks

With no job, no safety net, and no familiar faces, I packed up my Camaro and moved across the country to chase a new dream. It was a leap of faith that taught me the value of discomfort, growth, and relentless belief in one’s purpose.

03

Overcoming Challenges

Returning to the rigors of academia after three years away wasn’t easy. Rebuilding structure, discipline, and stamina took grit—but I did it while juggling contract work and laying the groundwork for the next chapter of my career.

Breakthrough Moment

Securing a pharmacy externship with the CDC was a pivotal breakthrough that affirmed my path and revealed my strength in translating complex clinical data into impactful public health solutions.

Current Chapter

Present Day Professional & Personal Focus
01

Where I Am Now

I translate the language of clinical pharmacy into the language of everyday life. At the intersection of healthcare and technology, I bridge worlds where computer programming meets pharmaceutical science. From public health to precision medicine, my journey has shown me that healthcare’s biggest challenges are often communication puzzles waiting to be solved.

02

Professional Philosophy

I believe that knowledge should be as accessible as medicine itself. My philosophy is simple: empower, simplify, and illuminate. Through design, data, and dialogue, I aim to transform complex health information into stories that inform, inspire, and drive change.

"

The greatest innovations in healthcare won't just come from new discoveries—they'll come from new ways of communicating what we already know.

03

Looking Forward

I’m on a mission to democratize healthcare knowledge through visual storytelling and technological innovation. By exploring the emerging frontiers of healthcare informatics, data visualization, and programming, I strive to turn raw clinical data into insights that improve lives—one chart, one line of code, one person at a time.

Next Chapter Preview
